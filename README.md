## Projects

---

### Habitat Mapping & Vegetation Analysis — Cloquet Forestry Center
I mapped out the vegetation zones at the Cloquet Forestry Center in 
northern Minnesota and classified tree stands by age — young growth, 
intermediate, and old. Layered in streams and roads to see how habitat 
was distributed relative to those features. Ended up calculating about 
486 hectares of prime habitat using attribute queries across multiple 
data layers.

---

### Visibility & Elevation Profiles
Ran a viewshed analysis to map what's visible versus not visible from a 
specific observer point across a stretch of terrain. Overlaid a hillshade 
to show the elevation and added an elevation profile graph so you could 
actually see the rise and drop in the landscape. It's one of those maps 
that makes terrain feel three dimensional.

---

### Brush Valley Creek Watershed — Root River, Houston County MN
Delineated the Brush Valley Creek watershed within the Root River basin 
in Houston County, Minnesota. Mapped the full stream network, watershed 
boundary, outlet point, and underlying elevation. Good for understanding 
how water moves through a landscape and where it drains to.

---

### Soil Fertility Classification — Macon County, North Carolina
Mapped soil types across Macon County and classified each one by 
fertility and drainage. So you can look at it and immediately see which 
areas have productive soil versus which ones drain poorly. Useful for 
conservation and land use planning.

---

### U.S. County Population Distribution
Pulled Census population data for every county across the contiguous 48 
states and mapped it using proportional symbols. The bigger the circle, 
the bigger the population. Simple but a good way to see just how 
concentrated people are on the coasts versus how spread out the middle 
of the country really is.

---

### High Population Counties with High Old/Young Age Ratios
Combined population data with age ratio data to find counties that were 
both densely populated and skewing older. The yellow highlights are the 
ones that met both criteria. Interesting to see where aging populations 
are actually concentrated versus just scattered.
